# KLE Case Study System

This system standardises how KLE captures, writes, and publishes proof content for SEO and conversion.

## A) Repeatable Case Study Template

Use this structure for every `/case-studies/[slug]` page.

### 1. Hero
- Client type and challenge in one line.
- Outcome headline with measurable result.
- Primary CTA: book a consultation.

### 2. Snapshot Panel
- Industry
- Company stage/size
- Engagement type (coaching, fractional, facilitation)
- Duration
- Core outcomes (3 bullets)

### 3. Situation (Before)
- What was happening operationally and culturally?
- What was at risk if nothing changed?

### 4. Tension / Real Problem
- What was the "elephant in the room"?
- What leadership or team dynamics blocked progress?

### 5. Intervention (What KLE Did)
- Diagnostic and alignment steps.
- Practical execution support.
- Coaching/fractional/facilitation cadence.

### 6. Actions (What Changed In Practice)
- New leadership behaviours.
- Team/process changes.
- Decision and execution rhythm changes.

### 7. Outcomes (After)
- Quantified results where possible.
- Qualitative shifts (confidence, alignment, ownership).
- Time-to-value statement.

### 8. Client Voice
- 1-2 direct quotes:
  - one on outcomes
  - one on KLE differentiation (candour + execution)

### 9. Why It Worked
- Brief section connecting outcomes to method in plain English.

### 10. CTA
- "If your team is facing similar challenges, book a consultation."

## B) Interview Question Bank (Mapped To Core Themes)

Use these in client interviews to generate strong case study source material.

### Theme 1: Speed To Value
1. What changed in the first 30 to 90 days?
2. What took less time than expected once KLE was involved?
3. If KLE had not been involved, what would likely have happened?

### Theme 2: Candour And Alignment
1. What difficult truths became clear during the engagement?
2. Were there conversations your leadership team had been avoiding?
3. How did team alignment change after those conversations?

### Theme 3: Execution Over Advice
1. What did KLE help execute directly, beyond recommendations?
2. Can you share one tactical activity that produced visible change?
3. How did the execution pace compare with previous advisors?

### Theme 4: Leadership Shift (Managing To Leading)
1. How did your role as leader change over the engagement?
2. What are you delegating now that you were handling before?
3. How did your team's confidence and ownership change?

### Theme 5: Team Quality / A-Player Standard
1. What did you realise about team composition during the work?
2. Were role changes, promotions, or hiring decisions made?
3. What changed in accountability and performance standards?

### Theme 6: ROI And Investment Confidence
1. What made the investment feel worthwhile?
2. Which business outcomes justified the spend?
3. What would you say to someone hesitant about cost?

## C) Editorial Rules For Strong Case Studies

- Lead with outcomes, not methodology acronyms.
- Use specific time windows (`first 90 days`, `quarter one`, etc.).
- Include one measurable indicator and one human-impact indicator.
- Keep each section concise and skimmable.
- End with a relevant service CTA and link to at least one related case.

## D) Case Study Metadata Template

- Meta title: `[Outcome] Case Study | Kothari Leadership`
- Meta description: `How [client type] achieved [result] through candid leadership coaching and execution support from KLE.`
- H1: `[Outcome] for [Client Type]`
- URL slug format: `/case-studies/[industry]-[challenge]-[outcome]`

## E) Recommended First Three Case Stories

1. Crisis integration and accelerated execution (90-day speed proof).
2. Founder transition from operator to strategic leader.
3. Leadership team restructuring to raise the A-player bar.
